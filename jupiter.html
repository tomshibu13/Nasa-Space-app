Jupiter is the largest planet in the solar system and the fifth planet from the Sun. It is a gas giant known for its immense size, powerful storms, and iconic Great Red Spot. Jupiter has a strong magnetic field and is surrounded by a complex system of rings and moons.

  • Size: Jupiter has a diameter of about 142,984 kilometers (88,846 miles), making it over 11 times wider than Earth. Its mass is 318 times that of Earth, making it by far the largest planet in the solar system.

  • Composition: Jupiter is primarily made of hydrogen (around 90%) and helium (about 10%), similar to the Sun. It lacks a solid surface and is considered a gas giant, with a possible small rocky core deep within.

  • Orbit: Jupiter orbits the Sun at an average distance of about 778 million kilometers (484 million miles). It takes about 12 Earth years to complete one orbit.
