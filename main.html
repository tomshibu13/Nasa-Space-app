Explore the Solar System

The solar system consists of the Sun and everything that orbits it, including the eight planets, their moons, dwarf planets, comets, and asteroids. Discover the fascinating details of each planet below.

The Sun

The Sun is the star at the center of our solar system. It is a nearly perfect sphere of hot plasma and provides the light and heat necessary for life on Earth.

Mercury

Mercury is the smallest planet in our solar system and closest to the Sun. It has a rocky body and is known for its extreme temperature fluctuations.

Venus

Venus is similar in structure to Earth but has a thick atmosphere filled with carbon dioxide, leading to a strong greenhouse effect.

Earth

Earth is the only planet known to support life. It has a diverse climate and a variety of ecosystems.

Mars

Mars, known as the Red Planet, is home to the largest volcano and canyon in the solar system. It has signs of ancient water flow.

Jupiter

Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system, known for its Great Red Spot, a giant storm, and its many moons.

Saturn

Saturn is famous for its stunning rings, made up of ice and rock particles. It is a gas giant with a complex atmosphere.

Uranus

Uranus is an ice giant with a unique tilt, causing it to roll along its orbit. Its blue color comes from methane in the atmosphere.

Neptune

Neptune is the farthest planet from the Sun. It is known for its intense blue color and strong winds, the fastest in the solar system.
