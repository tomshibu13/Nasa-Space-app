Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun and often referred to as the "Red Planet" due to its reddish appearance, which is caused by iron oxide (rust) on its surface. It is one of the most studied planets in the solar system because of its potential to have harbored life in the past

  • Size:Mars has a diameter of about 6,779 kilometers (4,212 miles), making it about half the size of Earth.

  • Orbit:Mars orbits the Sun at an average distance of about 228 million kilometers (142 million miles). A year on Mars lasts 687 Earth days, almost twice as long as a year on Earth.


  • Temperature:Temperature: Mars is much colder than Earth, with average surface temperatures of around -80°C (-112°F), although temperatures can vary widely depending on the location and time of day, ranging from 20°C (68°F) near the equator during the day to -125°C (-195°F) at the poles at night.
