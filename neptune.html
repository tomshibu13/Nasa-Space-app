Neptune is the eighth and farthest planet from the Sun in the solar system. It is a cold, windy, and dynamic world, known for its striking blue color due to the presence of methane in its atmosphere. Neptune is the fourth-largest planet by diameter and the third-largest by mass, following Jupiter and Saturn.

  • Mass:Neptune is 17 times more massive than Earth and is primarily composed of hydrogen, helium, and traces of water, ammonia, and methane ices.

  • Orbit:Neptune orbits the Sun at an average distance of about 4.5 billion kilometers (2.8 billion miles), making it the most distant planet. It takes about 165 Earth years to complete one orbit.

  • Temperature:The planet is incredibly cold, with temperatures at its cloud tops reaching around -214°C (-353°F). Despite its distance from the Sun, Neptune has an internal heat source that radiates more energy than it receives from the Sun.
