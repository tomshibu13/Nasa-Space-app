Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun and the third-largest planet in the solar system. It is classified as an "ice giant" because of the significant amounts of water, ammonia, and methane ices in its atmosphere and interior.

  • Size:Uranus has a diameter of about 50,724 kilometers (31,518 miles), making it slightly larger than Neptune but smaller than Jupiter and Saturn.

  • Mass:Uranus is about 14.5 times more massive than Earth but less dense than the ice giant Neptune.


  • Temperature:Uranus is one of the coldest planets in the solar system, with an average temperature of around -224°C (-371°F). Unlike other gas giants, Uranus emits very little internal heat, making its outer atmosphere exceptionally frigid.
